🌟 Quick Tips for New Expats
  • Register your residence with local authorities within 30 days of arrival
  • Carry a copy of your passport and visa at all times
  • Download the Careem or inDrive apps — reliable ride-hailing throughout Egypt
  • Swipe your tourist police contact (126) before you need it
  • Join our community to find other expats in your city
  • Many expats use Vodafone Egypt or Orange for best coverage nationwide

⚠️ Safety Tips for Expats
  • Always share your location with a trusted contact when traveling alone
  • Photograph your passport, visa, and residence permit — save copies to cloud storage
  • The Tourist Police (126) speak English and are specifically trained to assist foreigners
  • Use Uber or Careem instead of unmarked taxis, especially at night
  • Know your nearest embassy — they can issue emergency travel documents
  • Tap water is not recommended for drinking — use bottled water

🏠 Renting in Egypt — Expat Tips
  • Always notarize your lease at the local Shahr Aqari (Real Estate Registry) — "Tawtheeq"
  • Gated compounds offer the best security and maintenance — worth the premium
  • Maadi, Zamalek, New Cairo (5th Settlement), and Sheikh Zayed are most popular with expats
  • Ask your realtor about utilities setup — some landlords include them, others don't
  • A bilingual lawyer review of your contract is strongly recommended
  • Most apartments are rented unfurnished — budget for furniture separately
